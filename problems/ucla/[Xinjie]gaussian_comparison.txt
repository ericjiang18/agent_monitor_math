Let \(a_1,\dots,a_n\) be complex-valued random variables, and let
\[
a=(a_1,\dots,a_n)\in \mathbb{C}^n.
\]
Let \(\xi_1,\dots,\xi_n\) be i.i.d. random variables, each uniformly distributed on the complex unit circle, and let \(G_1,\dots,G_n\) be independent centered complex Gaussian random variables with covariance matrix \(\tfrac12 I_2\). Assume that the families
\[
(a_1,\dots,a_n), \qquad \xi_1,\dots,\xi_n, \qquad G_1,\dots,G_n
\]
are mutually independent.

Define
\[
S_n=\sum_{j=1}^n a_j\xi_j.
\]. 

You may assume that for every \(1 < t \leq \sqrt{2}\),
\[
\mathbb{P}\bigl(|S_n|\ge t\bigr)
\le
e\,\mathbb{P}\!\left(\left|\sum_{j=1}^n a_j G_j\right|\ge t\right).
\].

Prove that for every \(t \geq \sqrt{2}\),
\[
\mathbb{P}\bigl(|S_n|\ge t\bigr)
\le
e\,\mathbb{P}\!\left(\left|\sum_{j=1}^n a_j G_j\right|\ge t\right).
\]
