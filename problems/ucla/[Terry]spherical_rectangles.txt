Let A be a set of N points on the unit sphere in R^3.  How many rectangles (a_1,a_2,a_3,a_4) in A^4 are there with a_1+a_2=a_3+a_4?  In the literature there is a bound of O(N^{2+1/3}); a more obscure result in the literature improves this to O(N^{2+2/9+o(1)}).  We have an unpublished result of O(N^{2+1/7+o(1)}).  Tools such as the spherical Szemeredi-Trotter theorem are relevant.
