\begin{problem*}
Let \(n\geq 1\), and let
\[
\pi\colon S^n\longrightarrow \mathbb{RP}^n
\]
be the quotient map identifying antipodal points. Choose a basepoint
\(x_0\in S^n\), and use \([x_0]\in \mathbb{RP}^n\) as the basepoint of
\(\mathbb{RP}^n\), so that \(\pi\) is a based map.

Let \(\widetilde{\KO}^{*}\) denote reduced real \(K\)-theory. Consider the
induced map
\[
\pi^*\colon \widetilde{\KO}^{-1}(\mathbb{RP}^n)
\longrightarrow
\widetilde{\KO}^{-1}(S^n).
\]

Is its image contained in the subgroup of doubles in the target? That is,
is
\[
\operatorname{im}(\pi^*)\subseteq
2\,\widetilde{\KO}^{-1}(S^n),
\]
where
\[
2\,\widetilde{\KO}^{-1}(S^n)
=
\{\,2y : y\in \widetilde{\KO}^{-1}(S^n)\,\}?
\]
\end{problem*}
