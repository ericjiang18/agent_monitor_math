For all $\varepsilon > 0$ and $d \in \mathbb{Z}^+$, there exists $n_0 \in \mathbb{Z}^+$ such that the following holds for all integers $n > n_0$. Let $k$ be a positive integer satisfying $k > 2^{(\log n)^{1/2 + \varepsilon}}$, and let $p$ be a prime satisfying $p > 2^{n^{1+\varepsilon}}$. Let $X \subset \mathbb{F}_p^k$ be a random $d$ dimensional subspace. With probability at least $0.99$ over the choice of $X$, for all positive integers $h < n$ and for all subsets $S_1, \ldots, S_k \subset \mathbb{F}_p$ such that $\vert S_1\vert = \ldots = \vert S_k\vert = h$, we have \[\big\vert \{x \in X : x_1 \in S_1 \land \ldots \land x_k \in S_k\}\big\vert \leq (1+\varepsilon)h.\]
